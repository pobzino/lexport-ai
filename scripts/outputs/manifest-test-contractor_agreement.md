### Confidentiality

6.1 Confidentiality Obligations. Each Receiving Party will (a) hold the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information in strict confidence using at least the same degree of care it uses to protect its own confidential information but in no event less than reasonable care; (b) use the Confidential Information only as necessary to perform its obligations or exercise its rights under this Agreement; and (c) not disclose Confidential Information to any third party except to its employees, agents, contractors or professional advisors who have a need to know and who are bound by confidentiality obligations no less restrictive than those in this Agreement.\n\n6.2 Required Disclosure. If a Receiving Party is required by law, regulation, or valid legal process to disclose Confidential Information, it will (to the extent permitted) provide prompt written notice to the Disclosing Party to enable the Disclosing Party to seek protective orders or other appropriate remedies. The Receiving Party will disclose only that portion of the Confidential Information that it is legally required to disclose and will use reasonable efforts to obtain confidential treatment for such disclosures.\n\n6.3 Return or Destruction. Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement, or upon earlier written request, the Receiving Party will promptly return or destroy all Confidential Information of the Disclosing Party and provide written certification of destruction if requested, except that one copy may be retained for archival or compliance purposes subject to the confidentiality obligations herein.

A party seeking indemnification ("Indemnitee") will promptly notify the other party ("Indemnitor") in writing of any claim for which it seeks indemnity. Failure to give prompt notice will not relieve the Indemnitor of its obligations except to the extent the Indemnitor is materially prejudiced by the delay. The Indemnitor will have the right to assume and control the defense and settlement of any claim using counsel of its choice, provided that the Indemnitee may participate in the defense at its own expense. The Indemnitor may not settle any claim that admits liability or imposes any obligation on the Indemnitee without the Indemnitee's prior written consent, which consent shall not be unreasonably withheld or delayed.

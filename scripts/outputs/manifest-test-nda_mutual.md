### Definitions

1.1 "Confidential Information" means all information disclosed by or on behalf of a Disclosing Party to the Receiving Party, whether disclosed orally, visually, in writing, electronically, or by any other means, that is designated as confidential or that reasonably should be understood to be confidential given the nature of the information and the circumstances of disclosure. Confidential Information includes, without limitation, business plans, strategies, financial information, forecasts, projections, personnel information, customer and supplier lists, pricing, product designs, prototypes, specifications, software, source code, object code, inventions, know-how, trade secrets, marketing plans, and technical data, and any analyses, compilations, studies, notes, summaries, excerpts, or other derivatives of such information.

1.2 "Purpose" means evaluating and negotiating a potential business partnership between the Parties and related preliminary activities for a period described in Section 2.1.

1.3 "Term" means the period beginning on the Effective Date and continuing for the Duration specified herein.

1.4 "Affiliate" means any entity that, directly or indirectly, controls, is controlled by, or is under common control with a Party. For purposes of this definition, "control" means ownership of or the right to exercise more than fifty percent (50%) of the voting power of the applicable entity or the ability to direct its management or policies.

1.5 "Representatives" means a Party's and its Affiliates' directors, officers, employees, contractors, agents, attorneys, accountants, consultants and other advisors who have a need to know the Confidential Information for the Purpose. Representatives also include potential financers, investors or acquirers who are considering a transaction with a Party, provided such persons agree to be bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as those in this Agreement.

1.6 "Disclosing Party" means the Party or its Affiliate that discloses Confidential Information. "Receiving Party" means the Party or its Affiliate that receives Confidential Information.

### Term; Duration

2.1 This Agreement commences on the Effective Date and continues for a period of two (2) years (the "Duration") unless earlier terminated by mutual written agreement. Confidentiality obligations with respect to Confidential Information disclosed during the Term shall survive as provided in Section 10.

2.2 Notwithstanding termination of this Agreement, any Confidential Information that constitutes a trade secret under applicable law shall be protected for so long as such information remains a trade secret.

### Exclusions from Confidential Information

3.1 Confidential Information does not include information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate by competent evidence: (a) was in the public domain at the time of disclosure or thereafter entered the public domain other than by breach of this Agreement by the Receiving Party or its Representatives; (b) was lawfully known to the Receiving Party prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party, as shown by the Receiving Party's written records; (c) was rightfully received by the Receiving Party from a third party without breach of an obligation of confidentiality and without restriction on disclosure; or (d) was independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information, as evidenced by contemporaneous written records.

3.2 For clarity, combinations or compilations of elements of Confidential Information may themselves be protected as Confidential Information, even where individual elements are publicly known or otherwise excluded, to the extent the combination or compilation (i) is not in the public domain and (ii) derives independent value from the non-public selection or arrangement of such elements. The Receiving Party bears the burden of proving by competent evidence any asserted exclusion.

### Confidentiality Obligations

4.1 The Receiving Party shall: (a) hold the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information in strict confidence using at least the same degree of care it uses to protect its own Confidential Information of a similar nature, but in no event less than reasonable care; (b) not disclose such Confidential Information to any third party except as permitted by this Agreement; and (c) use the Confidential Information solely to exercise rights and perform obligations in connection with the Purpose.

4.2 The Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information only to its Representatives who have a need to know for the Purpose and who are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as those in this Agreement. The Receiving Party remains responsible for any breach of this Agreement by its Representatives.

4.3 The Receiving Party shall promptly notify the Disclosing Party in writing upon becoming aware of any unauthorized use or disclosure of the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information and shall cooperate with the Disclosing Party to prevent or remedy such unauthorized use or disclosure.

### Return or Destruction of Confidential Information

5.1 Upon the Disclosing Party's written request (which request may be made at any time during the Term or within thirty (30) days following termination or expiration of this Agreement), the Receiving Party shall, within thirty (30) days of receipt of such request: (a) promptly return to the Disclosing Party all originals and copies of the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information in the Receiving Party's possession or control or (b) at the Disclosing Party's option, destroy such Confidential Information and provide a written certification signed by an officer or authorized representative of the Receiving Party that all such Confidential Information has been returned or destroyed, except as expressly set forth in Section 5.2.

5.2 Notwithstanding the foregoing, the Receiving Party may retain one (1) archival copy of the Confidential Information to the extent required by its legal, regulatory or internal document retention policies (including routine back-up systems); provided that any such retained Confidential Information shall remain subject to all confidentiality obligations under this Agreement and the Receiving Party will not use such retained Confidential Information for any purpose other than for compliance with applicable retention obligations and shall take commercially reasonable steps to isolate and protect such retained Confidential Information from further use or disclosure.

5.3 The obligation to return or destroy Confidential Information does not apply to (a) materials that are integrated into the Receiving Party's internal records solely for archival, compliance or disaster recovery purposes and (b) work product or materials created independently by the Receiving Party that do not incorporate the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information. Any retained materials described in this Section 5.3 shall continue to be treated as Confidential Information if they incorporate or derive from the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information.

### Permitted Disclosures; Compelled Disclosure

6.1 The Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information to the extent required by applicable law, rule, regulation or by order of a court or governmental authority of competent jurisdiction (a "Compelled Disclosure").

6.2 In the event of a Compelled Disclosure, the Receiving Party shall, to the extent permitted by law, (a) provide the Disclosing Party with prompt written notice of the demand for disclosure so that the Disclosing Party may seek a protective order or other appropriate remedy, and (b) cooperate with the Disclosing Party's reasonable efforts to obtain such protective order or other remedy at the Disclosing Party's expense. If disclosure is required and no protective order or other remedy is obtained, the Receiving Party may disclose only that portion of the Confidential Information that it is legally required to disclose and shall exercise reasonable efforts to obtain confidential treatment for any Confidential Information so disclosed.

6.3 The Receiving Party shall use reasonable efforts to notify affected third parties (including the Disclosing Party) when required to disclose their Confidential Information pursuant to compulsory process, unless prohibited by law or a court order.

### Injunctive Relief; Remedies

7.1 The Parties acknowledge that monetary damages may be inadequate to fully remedy a breach or threatened breach of this Agreement and that the Disclosing Party may suffer irreparable harm. Accordingly, in addition to any other remedies available at law or in equity, the Disclosing Party shall be entitled to seek injunctive relief, specific performance or other equitable relief to prevent or curtail any actual or threatened breach of this Agreement, without the necessity of posting bond.

7.2 Nothing in this Agreement limits the right of either Party to pursue any other remedies available under applicable law for breaches of this Agreement, including monetary damages, provided that any recovery shall be reduced by any amounts that fairly compensate the non-breaching Party for benefits retained as a consequence of the breach.

### Non-Solicitation (Optional)

8.1 During the Term and for a period of twelve (12) months following the termination or expiration of this Agreement, neither Party shall directly solicit for employment or engagement any employee of the other Party who was involved in the discussions or who had access to Confidential Information, unless the solicited employee responds to a general public job posting or independently approaches the soliciting Party without any direct solicitation. This Section 8.1 shall not prohibit general recruitment campaigns not specifically targeted at the other Party's employees.

8.2 The Parties agree that the restrictions in this Section 8 are reasonable and necessary to protect legitimate business interests. If any jurisdiction limits the enforceability of this Section 8, the Parties agree to enforce it to the maximum extent permitted.

9.2 Governing Law; Venue. This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of California, without regard to its conflict of laws principles. The Parties agree that the state and federal courts located in the County of San Francisco, California shall have exclusive jurisdiction and venue over any dispute arising out of or relating to this Agreement.

9.3 Attorneys' Fees. In the event of any action to enforce this Agreement, the prevailing Party shall be entitled to recover its reasonable attorneys' fees and costs from the non-prevailing Party, in addition to any other relief granted.

9.4 Assignment. Neither Party may assign or transfer this Agreement or any rights or obligations hereunder without the prior written consent of the other Party, except that a Party may assign this Agreement to an Affiliate or to a successor in interest in connection with a merger, acquisition, corporate reorganization or sale of all or substantially all of its assets, provided that the assignee assumes the assigning Party's obligations under this Agreement.

9.5 Entire Agreement; Amendment. This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties with respect to the subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior and contemporaneous agreements and understandings, whether written or oral. This Agreement may be amended only by a written instrument signed by authorized representatives of both Parties.

9.6 Severability; Waiver. If any provision of this Agreement is held to be invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall remain in full force and effect. The failure of either Party to enforce any right under this Agreement shall not constitute a waiver of that right.

9.7 No License; No Obligation. Except as expressly set forth herein, no license or other rights, by implication, estoppel or otherwise, are granted under any patents, copyrights, trade secrets or other intellectual property rights of either Party. Nothing in this Agreement obligates either Party to proceed with any transaction or relationship.

9.8 Relationship of the Parties. The Parties are independent contractors and nothing in this Agreement creates the relationship of partners, joint venturers, employees or agents, except as otherwise expressly agreed in writing.

9.9 Counterparts; Electronic Signatures. This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original, and all of which together shall constitute one and the same instrument. Signatures transmitted by facsimile or electronic means (including PDF) shall be deemed original signatures.

### Survival

10.1 All obligations of confidentiality and non-use with respect to Confidential Information disclosed during the Term shall survive termination or expiration of this Agreement for a period of three (3) years after the date of disclosure, except that Confidential Information that constitutes a trade secret under applicable law shall continue to be protected for so long as it remains a trade secret.

10.2 Sections concerning Return or Destruction (Section 5), Injunctive Relief; Remedies (Section 7), Governing Law; Venue; Attorneys' Fees (Section 9.2 and 9.3), and this Section 10 shall survive termination or expiration of this Agreement.

2.2 Scope. The assignment expressly includes all rights to: (a) claim, apply for and pursue patents, patent applications and inventions related to the Work; (b) claim, register, maintain and enforce copyrights and related rights; (c) assert, maintain and protect trade secret rights and to control access to and disclosure of trade secrets; and (d) sue or join with Assignee in suing for past, present and future infringements, misappropriations, or other violations of the Assigned Rights and to recover damages, profits and other remedies.

2.3 Further Assurances. Assignor shall, at Assignee's expense, execute and deliver such further instruments and take such further actions as reasonably requested by Assignee to effect, record or confirm the assignments and rights granted in this Agreement.

3.3 Non-Infringement (Knowledge-Based). Assignor represents and warrants, to the best of his knowledge after reasonable inquiry, that the Work and the Intellectual Property as provided to Assignee do not knowingly infringe, misappropriate or violate any third party's intellectual property rights.

3.4 No Pending Claims. Assignor further represents that he is not aware of any pending claim, demand or proceeding by any third party challenging Assignor's ownership of the Work or asserting that the Work infringes any third party rights, except as disclosed in writing to Assignee prior to the Effective Date.

### Representations and Warranties of Assignee

4.1 Authority. Assignee represents and warrants that it has full power and authority to enter into this Agreement and to perform its obligations hereunder.

4.2 No Conflict. Assignee represents and warrants that the execution, delivery and performance of this Agreement by Assignee does not and will not conflict with or result in a breach of any material agreement, obligation or instrument to which Assignee is a party or by which it is bound.

4.3 Business Purpose and Diligence. Assignee acknowledges that it has had the opportunity to conduct its own due diligence regarding the Work and has negotiated the terms of this Agreement with knowledge of its business purpose for acquiring the Intellectual Property. Assignee accepts the assignment based on its own examination and not in reliance on any representation other than those expressly set forth herein.

### Consideration; Payment

5.1 Purchase Price. In consideration for the assignment of the Assigned Rights, Assignee shall pay to Assignor the total purchase price of Fifty Thousand Dollars (US$50,000) (the "Purchase Price").

5.2 Payment Terms. The Purchase Price shall be paid in full no later than fifteen (15) business days following the Effective Date by wire transfer to a bank account designated in writing by Assignor or by another mutually agreed method. Receipt of funds by Assignor constitutes full and final payment.

5.3 Taxes. Each party shall be responsible for its own income and payroll taxes arising from the transactions contemplated by this Agreement. Assignor shall be solely responsible for any taxes, withholdings or similar amounts owed in connection with the receipt of the Purchase Price, except that Assignee shall withhold or report as required by law and shall provide Assignor with appropriate tax reporting documentation (e.g., Form 1099) as applicable.

5.4 No Other Consideration. Except as expressly set forth in this Agreement, Assignor acknowledges that no other consideration, payments, royalties or future compensation are owed by Assignee in connection with the Assigned Rights.

### Confidentiality

6.1 Confidential Information. For purposes of this Agreement, "Confidential Information" means all non-public information disclosed by either party to the other in connection with the Work or this Agreement, including without limitation the Work itself, source code, designs, trade secrets, specifications, business plans, customer lists and pricing.

6.2 Use and Non-Disclosure. Each party shall (a) use Confidential Information of the other party only as reasonably necessary to perform its obligations under this Agreement or as authorized in writing; and (b) not disclose Confidential Information to any third party except as expressly permitted in this Agreement.

6.3 Standard of Care. Each party shall protect the Confidential Information of the other party using at least the same degree of care that it uses to protect its own similar confidential information, but in no event less than a reasonable standard of care.

6.4 Exclusions. Confidential Information does not include information that: (a) is or becomes publicly available other than through a breach of this Agreement; (b) was lawfully in the receiving party's possession prior to disclosure by the disclosing party as evidenced by written records; (c) is rightfully received from a third party without restriction and without breach of a confidentiality obligation; or (d) is independently developed by the receiving party without use of or reference to the disclosing party's Confidential Information, as evidenced by contemporaneous written records.

6.5 Compelled Disclosure. If a receiving party is required by law, regulation or court order to disclose Confidential Information, it shall provide prompt written notice to the disclosing party to allow the disclosing party to seek protective measures and shall disclose only that portion of Confidential Information legally required to be disclosed.

7.1 Moral Rights Waiver. To the maximum extent permitted by applicable law, Assignor hereby irrevocably waives and agrees not to assert any moral rights (including rights of paternity, integrity, disclosure or withdrawal) in the Intellectual Property and any modifications, adaptations or derivative works created by or for Assignee.

7.2 Consent. Assignor hereby irrevocably consents to any action by Assignee or its licensees or successors that would otherwise implicate moral rights.

7.3 Attribution. Nothing in this Agreement requires Assignee to provide attribution to Assignor in connection with its exploitation of the Intellectual Property, and Assignor hereby waives any right to require attribution, except as otherwise agreed in writing.

### Indemnification

8.1 Assignor Indemnity. Assignor shall indemnify, defend and hold harmless Assignee and its officers, directors, employees, agents and affiliates (collectively, the "Assignee Indemnitees") from and against any and all losses, liabilities, damages, costs and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising out of or resulting from a third-party claim to the extent such claim is based upon: (a) Assignor's breach of any representation, warranty or covenant in this Agreement; or (b) a claim that the Work, as delivered by Assignor prior to the Effective Date, infringes, misappropriates or otherwise violates a third party's intellectual property or proprietary rights, but only to the extent such infringement arises from Assignor's actions or content provided by Assignor.

8.2 Assignee Indemnity (Optional, Post-Transfer Exploitation). Assignee shall indemnify, defend and hold harmless Assignor from and against any and all losses, liabilities, damages, costs and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising from Assignor's use of the Intellectual Property after the Effective Date that is not authorized by this Agreement, provided that such indemnity shall not apply to claims arising from Assignor's breaches occurring prior to the Effective Date.

8.3 Procedure. The indemnified party (the "Indemnitee") shall promptly notify the indemnifying party (the "Indemnitor") in writing of any claim for which indemnity is sought. The Indemnitor shall have the right to assume control of the defense and settlement of such claim with counsel of its choice reasonably satisfactory to the Indemnitee; provided that the Indemnitor shall not settle any claim if the settlement would impose any monetary obligation or admit fault on the Indemnitee without the Indemnitee's prior written consent, which shall not be unreasonably withheld. The Indemnitee shall cooperate reasonably in the defense and may, at its own expense, participate in the defense with counsel of its choosing.

### Limitation of Liability

9.1 Exclusion of Consequential Damages. Except for liability arising from a party's gross negligence, willful misconduct, fraud or a party's breach of its confidentiality or indemnification obligations, neither party shall be liable to the other for any special, indirect, incidental, consequential, exemplary or punitive damages (including lost profits, lost business or loss of data), however caused and whether in contract, tort (including negligence), strict liability or otherwise, even if the party has been advised of the possibility of such damages.

9.2 Liability Cap. Except for (a) liability arising from a party's gross negligence, willful misconduct, fraud, or (b) a party's indemnification obligations under Section 8 or breach of confidentiality under Section 6, the aggregate liability of either party to the other arising out of or relating to this Agreement shall not exceed the Purchase Price paid under this Agreement (US$50,000).

9.3 Allocation of Risk. The parties acknowledge that the limitations and exclusions of liability in this Agreement reflect the allocation of risk between the parties and are an essential basis of the bargain, and that the Purchase Price reflects such allocation.

10.4 Effect of Termination. Termination shall not rescind or unwind the assignment effected by Section 2, which is intended to be irrevocable and effective as of the Effective Date; termination shall only affect future obligations under this Agreement as expressly provided.

### Definitions

1.1 "Agreement" means this One-Way Non-Disclosure Agreement.
1.2 "Effective Date" means the date on which the last Party executes this Agreement.
1.3 "Confidential Information" means any and all information or materials disclosed by or on behalf of Disclosing Party to Receiving Party, whether disclosed orally, in writing, visually, electronically, or by inspection of tangible objects, that is designated as confidential or that reasonably should be understood to be confidential given the nature of the information and the circumstances of disclosure. Confidential Information includes, without limitation, business information, strategic plans, financial data, technical data, product designs, prototypes, inventions, processes, formulas, software (including source and object code), algorithms, models, research and development, trade secrets, customer lists and information, supplier and vendor information, pricing, marketing and sales information, and any analyses, compilations, studies, notes, summaries or other material prepared by Receiving Party that contain or are based upon such information. 1.4 The existence of the discussions between the Parties, and the terms, conditions, existence, status and content of this Agreement and any negotiations or proposed transaction between the Parties, shall be treated as Confidential Information to the extent permitted by law.

### Exclusions from Confidential Information

2.1 The obligations in this Agreement do not apply to any information to the extent that Receiving Party can establish by written records: (a) was in the public domain at the time of disclosure or later entered the public domain through no fault or breach by Receiving Party; (b) was rightfully in Receiving Party's possession prior to receipt from Disclosing Party as evidenced by written records; (c) was lawfully obtained from a third party who had the right to disclose such information and who did not obtain it directly or indirectly from Disclosing Party; or (d) was independently developed by Receiving Party without use of or reference to Disclosing Party's Confidential Information, as demonstrated by Receiving Party's written contemporaneous records. 2.2 The burden of proving that any exclusion applies rests with Receiving Party and must be established by written records. 2.3 Notwithstanding any other provision of this Agreement, Receiving Party shall have no right to rely on or assert a "residual knowledge" exception that would permit use of or disclosure of Confidential Information based solely on information retained in unaided memory after exposure. Any use, retention or disclosure must comply with this Agreement.

### Disclosure and Use Restrictions

3.1 Receiving Party shall: (a) hold and maintain the Confidential Information in strict confidence using at least the same degree of care as it uses to protect its own confidential information of like importance, but in no event less than a reasonable degree of care; (b) use Confidential Information solely for the Purpose of evaluating a potential consulting engagement and for no other purpose without the prior written consent of Disclosing Party; and (c) not disclose Confidential Information to any third party except as permitted by this Agreement. 3.2 Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information only to its employees, agents, contractors, advisors and representatives (collectively, "Representatives") who have a need to know for the Purpose and who are bound by written confidentiality obligations no less restrictive than those in this Agreement; provided, however, Receiving Party will remain liable for any breach by its Representatives. 3.3 Receiving Party will not reverse engineer, disassemble, decompile or otherwise attempt to derive source code, underlying ideas, algorithms, or trade secrets from any Confidential Information.

### Return or Destruction of Confidential Information

4.1 Upon Disclosing Party's written request or upon termination of discussions between the Parties, Receiving Party shall promptly, and in any event within ten (10) business days after receipt of such request or termination, return to Disclosing Party all documents and other materials (and all copies thereof) containing or representing Confidential Information, or, at Disclosing Party's option, destroy such materials and provide Disclosing Party with a written certification signed by an officer or authorized representative of Receiving Party confirming the return or destruction. 4.2 If Receiving Party destroys Confidential Information, it shall destroy all electronic copies and erase the information from its electronic systems to the extent reasonably practicable, except to the limited extent retention is required by applicable law or by Receiving Party's written records retention policies, in which case Receiving Party shall continue to treat such retained Confidential Information as confidential under this Agreement. 4.3 The obligations under this Section 4 are in addition to, and not in lieu of, any equitable rights Disclosing Party may have.

### Compelled Disclosure

5.1 If Receiving Party or any of its Representatives is compelled by law, rule, regulation, subpoena, court order or other legal process to disclose Confidential Information, Receiving Party shall, to the extent legally permitted, promptly notify Disclosing Party in writing so that Disclosing Party may seek a protective order or other appropriate remedy. 5.2 If Disclosing Party does not obtain a protective order or other remedy and Receiving Party is legally compelled to disclose Confidential Information, Receiving Party may disclose only that portion of the Confidential Information that Receiving Party is advised by written opinion of counsel is legally required to disclose. 5.3 Receiving Party shall, to the extent legally permissible, cooperate with Disclosing Party (at Disclosing Party's expense) in seeking such protective order or other remedy and in any related proceedings, and shall use reasonable efforts to obtain assurances that confidential treatment will be accorded to the Confidential Information.

### Term; Survival

6.1 The term of this Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and continue for three (3) years (the "Term"); provided, however, the obligations of confidentiality with respect to any Confidential Information shall survive termination or expiration of this Agreement for a period of three (3) years from the date of last disclosure of Confidential Information, except for trade secrets, for which obligations shall survive for as long as such information remains a trade secret under applicable law. 6.2 Notwithstanding the foregoing, any and all obligations with respect to trade secrets and any other Confidential Information requiring longer protection under applicable law shall survive for the period required by such law.

### Remedies; Injunctive Relief

7.1 Receiving Party acknowledges that any unauthorized disclosure or use of Confidential Information may cause irreparable harm to Disclosing Party for which monetary damages may be an inadequate remedy. 7.2 Accordingly, in addition to any other remedies available at law or in equity, Disclosing Party shall be entitled to seek injunctive relief, specific performance, or other equitable remedies to prevent or curtail any actual or threatened breach of this Agreement. 7.3 Nothing in this Agreement limits Disclosing Party's right to seek injunctive or other equitable relief in any court of competent jurisdiction. The Parties agree that seeking injunctive relief in a jurisdiction other than the exclusive venue specified in Section 9 is a carve-out that does not waive other venue provisions of this Agreement.

### No Warranty; No Obligation

8.1 All Confidential Information is provided "AS IS" without any representation or warranty, express or implied, as to its accuracy or completeness. 8.2 Nothing in this Agreement obligates either Party to proceed with any proposed transaction or business relationship, and each Party reserves the right, in its sole discretion, to terminate discussions at any time.

### Governing Law; Venue; Jurisdiction
